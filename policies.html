üìä Grading Philosophy: "Un-grading"

In this class, we won't be grading individual assignments or calculating points/percentages. Instead, we'll offer lots of feedback without any points or letter grades‚Äîbut we will indicate if you are meeting expectations.

How It Works

Throughout the semester, you will write three self-reflection essays (about a page each) on how you've developed as a learner. On the second and third essays, you will indicate the grade you believe you have earned. These will determine your midterm and final letter grades.

Why Un-grading?

This approach focuses on learning rather than performance, encouraging deeper engagement with the material. For more information, we recommend Jesse Stommel's blog posts: "Why I Don't Grade" and "How to Ungrade."

Grading Scale

A / A- Excellent (4.00 / 3.67)
B+ / B / B- Good (3.33 / 3.00 / 2.67)
C+ / C / C- Satisfactory (2.33 / 2.00 / 1.67)
D+ / D / D- Poor (1.33 / 1.00 / 0.67)
F Failing (0.00)

üìÖ Attendance & Tardiness

In keeping with our "un-grading" policy, we will not take official attendance nor deduct points for missed classes or late arrivals. However, the class moves quickly, and missing content affects your learning progression.

Your Responsibility

  • Remember that Monday's content is essential for Wednesday's work

Excused Absences

Our policy aligns with TCU's non-penalizing approach to Official University Absences, including:

  • Religious holy days
  • University-related absences

Please let us know in advance if you anticipate being absent, late, or leaving early!
