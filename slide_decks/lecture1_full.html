Douglas Rushkoff: Capabilities

"[T]he underlying capability of the computer era is actually programming—which almost none of us know how to do. We simply use the programs that have been made for us, and enter our text in the appropriate box on the screen. We teach kids how to use software to write, but not how to write software. This means they have access to the capabilities given to them by others, but not the power to determine the value-creating capabilities of these technologies for themselves."
Douglas Rushkoff, qtd in Nick Montfort's Exploratory Programming for the Arts and Humanities, 327-28

Facebook Account Gender Binaries

Facebook continues to resolve users' genders into a binary: either "male" or "female." This decision was made so that Facebook could allow its primary clients—advertisers—to more easily market to one gender or the other. Even if you can choose the gender that you show to your Facebook friends, you can't change the gender that Facebook provides to its paying customers.
Source: https://data-feminism.mitpress.mit.edu/pub/h1w0nbqp/release/3

Positive Voices Survey

A recent alternative to the binary, developed by Public Health England in collaboration with LGBTQ+ organizations in the United Kingdom. This two-item questionnaire was designed for use in routine national surveillance of HIV in England and Wales to determine self-identified gender and cis or trans status. The designers offer three named genders, a catch-all fourth category, and an option for not disclosing gender identity.
Source: https://data-feminism.mitpress.mit.edu/pub/h1w0nbqp/release/3
