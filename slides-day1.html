What is Digital Humanities?

Digital Humanities is not about replacing traditional methods‚Äîit's about expanding our toolkit for understanding human culture.

Traditional Humanities

  • Close reading of texts
  • Historical analysis
  • Cultural interpretation
  • Qualitative research
  • Narrative storytelling
  • Individual expertise

Digital Methods

  • Computational text analysis
  • Data visualization
  • Network analysis
  • Digital archives
  • Interactive narratives
  • Collaborative research

The goal: Use both approaches together to ask deeper questions about human experience.

Why Code in the Humanities?

Coding gives us superpowers for cultural analysis:

  • Scale: Analyze thousands of documents at once (imagine reading every 19th-century novel!)
  • Pattern Recognition: Discover hidden connections across time and space
  • Visualization: Make abstract concepts tangible and shareable
  • Preservation: Digitize and protect cultural heritage for future generations
  • Accessibility: Make knowledge more democratic and inclusive
  • New Questions: Ask what wasn't possible before technology
  • Collaboration: Work across disciplines and institutions
"We're not just using computers to do humanities faster‚Äîwe're using them to do humanities differently."
